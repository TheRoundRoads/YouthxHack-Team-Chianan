Brought in

Fish are brought in as larvae and are introduced to Singapore Aquaculture Technologies when they become fingerlings.

Fishery

They are added to a closed-containment system that allows for higher yields and precise control of various elements like oxygen levels, waste and feed.

The closed containment system uses video analytics to track behaviour like swimming patterns and skin lesions, ensuring fish harvested are of the highest quality.

Biofilters are used to break down waste products like ammonia into nitrogen as well as preventing overfeeding.

Additionally, 50% of the farms use solar energy to run its operations.

Supermarket

It takes about 8-10 months for the fishes to be ready for sale at the SAT.
